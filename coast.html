Trails

01 Pictured Rocks MI, US

Yeah, pictured rocks again! The 41.2 mile path to hike there is in the forest, also along the shore of the largest and northernmost of the Great Lakes of North America, Lake Superior. When you hike, right by your side is the deep blue lake with a gradient of shade. Your ears are filled with the sound of waves crawling gently to the shore. Camping is highly recommended. Although, it may be chilly during late October, bring a down jacket and a quality sleeping bag.

02 Dongxiyong Shenzhen, China

Dongxiyong is my favorite hiking trail in Shenzhen, where I spent three years as an undergraduate. It takes about 3 hours to complete the 5-mile long cross-coastal route. You can see the sea-eroded landforms such as beaches, islands, reefs, caves, and bridges while hiking along the rugged coastline. The landscape here will amaze anyone interested in geology. Remember to bring sunglasses and sunscreen, the sun is always scorching in southern China.

03 Great Ocean Walk Apollo Bay, Australia

I have this on my bucket list. The Great Ocean Walk shadows the iconic Great Ocean Road. It would be astonishing to weave the way through tall forests, coastal heathlands and beside rocky shore platforms, with panoramic views from windswept cliff-tops.
